In a world dominated by social platforms, having your own website might seem old-fashioned. I think it's more important than ever.

## Ownership

When you publish on someone else's platform, you're a guest. Algorithms change, accounts get suspended, and platforms shut down. Your own site is **yours**.

## Simplicity

A static site is:

- **Fast** — no server-side rendering, no database queries
- **Secure** — no attack surface beyond static files
- **Cheap** — free hosting on GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Cloudflare Pages
- **Durable** — HTML files work everywhere, forever

## A Creative Outlet

Building and maintaining a personal site is a small creative project that compounds over time. Every post, every tweak to the design — it all adds up.

If you've been thinking about making your own site, just start. It doesn't have to be perfect.
